Faces of Scattered Love is a title for the year-long series of projects where migratory birds flying to Arctic, small leaves filling the tundra horizon, and paper beings descending staircases converge.

The work unfolded through many methods across the year: mapping bird migrations and the arctic circle, drawing birds for animations, making patterns for the small leaves in tundra, designing architecture at the edge of horizon, inventing ten fictional characters with different professions for ten different visions, making paper beings, staging performances where these beings wandered through buildings as they wished, writing essays and poems, and producing books that gather them all.

They brought me these perspectives: mapping the shift boundaries beyond borders, watching the horizon with the midnight sun hanging just above it while noticing small leaves beneath the hand, and being overwhelmed by countless beings, losing count by facing them. This page gathers images that arrived from those perspectives.
